# LORI-MAGSHIELD

## Overview
LORI-MAGSHIELD is a localized magnetic shielding system designed to protect human habitats, critical infrastructure, and equipment from solar radiation and charged particle exposure in space environments such as the Moon and Mars.

Unlike planetary-scale protection concepts, this system focuses on **directional and localized defense**, redirecting charged particles rather than attempting full environmental shielding.

---

## Core Concept
The system is based on three key principles:

1. **Localized Protection**
   - Protect only what is necessary (humans, habitats, critical systems)
   - Reduces energy requirements significantly

2. **Magnetic Deflection (Not Blocking)**
   - Uses controlled magnetic fields to redirect solar wind particles
   - Avoids unrealistic "barrier" assumptions

3. **Modular Architecture**
   - Scalable from equipment-level to base-level deployment
   - Adaptable across lunar, Martian, and deep-space environments

---

## System Architecture (High-Level)

- Outer Layer: Electromagnetic field generation (superconducting coils or current loops)
- Control Layer: Dynamic field adjustment system (directional response to solar events)
- Inner Layer: Protected zone (humans, equipment, infrastructure)
- Optional Stabilization Layer: Ferromagnetic materials (iron/nickel) for localized field shaping

---

## Use Cases

- Lunar surface habitats
- Mars base protection systems
- Space stations and deep-space missions
- Critical equipment shielding in high-radiation zones

---

## Design Philosophy

> Do not protect everything.  
> Protect what matters.

This system reflects a shift from planetary engineering to **targeted survival infrastructure**, enabling realistic deployment under constrained energy and resource conditions.
